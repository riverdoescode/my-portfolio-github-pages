## *Beginnings*
This is not my first day. This story takes a full year to concede, and follows the elaborate manipulations of social constructs and theoretical amalgamations of Wreck-It-Ralph. Back to a week ago, it goes like every other day, scrolling indeed with a second monitor of youtube that autoplays into AceRola's Ray Tracing video log. While I could go on about AceRola's incredible ray tracing real time game, the brief introduction to *Path Tracing* drew my eye. Billions of calculations for millions of pixels in realistic approximation that repeats every single frame. 

Then ***Graphics Programming***. Art and math to create art possible in ways unforeseen? Two of the only things I was good at in school?! Sign me up! Hence the purpose of this blog, to catalog and record my journey to Graphics Programming.

As I see it, the pathways to the illustrious title of Graphics Programmer and few and blocked by the pristine pay locked college bricks. AceRola's path followed an associates in chemistry, bachelors in Computer Science with 2 intensive summers of graphics programming studies and eventually a job offer at Sony. Now here is my issue.
- My math is very limited.
- My coding know-how in limited to gdscript (Godot) and a little HTML.
- I'm broke. 
## *Mindset*
Learning is consistently difficult for me. Trauma formed education into a consistent survival method. My battlefield was the only safe room in a house of nightmares, the smoke choked at what passion for knowledge I had. This complied with ADHD, Autism, Anxiety, and a slight video game addiction has morphed my entire education pipeline into Action --> Reward. 

I cannot sustain a career of impulse. If my action is only driven by random impulses, I'll never form the habits needed for a computing and artistic career. In school we are taught about a growth mindset, which is sort of what I'm looking for. I don't much need to feel positive about myself as I need to train the erratic impulses of my current state. I've found a few resources for this, the most solid from Dr.K, who notes meditation and forcing impulse control with redirecting attention will help me out. 

Writing this blog will be my accountability. An open realtime log of everything I do, posted weekly. As Github is static, you'll just have to trust me on this.
